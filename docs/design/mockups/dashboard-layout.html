Key Design Decisions

Row 1 — Composite Readiness Score + Today's Session. The score answers "how am I doing?" and the session answers "what do I do today?" — the two questions every athlete has when they open the app. The readiness score is a weighted composite of sleep, recovery, nutrition, and training strain from wearable + daily log data. The traffic-light label (Train as Programmed / Reduce Volume / Deload / Rest) comes directly from the coaching team's decision matrix.

Row 2 — Week Overview + Protocol Compliance. The week strip shows context — what's done, what's today, what's coming. Compliance bars are dynamic, generated from whatever protocols the coaching team prescribed for this athlete. Your athlete might have "Lights Out" and "Mobility Work." A marathon runner might have "Foam Roll" and "Carb Loading."

Row 3 — Trend Sparklines. The 4 metrics shown here are selected by the coaching team based on what matters most for this athlete's current phase. For you right now it might be Weight, Sleep, Protein, Pull-ups. During taper it might shift to Recovery, HRV, Calories, Sleep. Tapping any card navigates to the full Trends page filtered to that metric.

Everything fits in one scroll. Progressive disclosure — tap to expand, tap to navigate deeper. The dashboard is a launchpad, not a data dump.
