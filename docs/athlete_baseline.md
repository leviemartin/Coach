## PART 2: TACTICAL CATEGORIES & COACH PROTOCOLS
*This section translates the raw intake into strict operational rules and fixes for the training plan.*

### A. Logistical Constraints
- **Family Priority:** Training and nutrition must integrate with the family, not isolate the athlete. Dinners are shared. Toddler sleep regressions will impact recovery; training must adjust to extreme fatigue but not be skipped.
- **Gym Window:** Primary training occurs at TrainMore, starting at 07:45 AM.
- **Meal Prep Workflow:** The athlete cooks for two days at a time to mitigate family friction. "Future You thanks Sunday You."
- **The Vizsla Protocol:** The athlete owns a Vizsla (an elite endurance breed). The dog is the designated "Pacer." All active recovery walks and weekend Rucks (90+ minutes, weighted) mandate the dog's presence.

### B. Nutritional Anchors & Fixes
- **The "Dad Dinner" Fix:** Component cooking. The family eats the same meal, but the athlete alters the ratio. Plate is 50% protein source, 25% veggies/greens, 25% carbs (rice/pasta/potatoes).
- **The WFH "Buldak Hack":** Buldak noodles are retained as an acceptable high-sodium post-workout/carb source, heavily modified: Drain water, add 200g of pre-cooked shredded chicken breast (or tuna), and a large handful of raw spinach. It transitions from junk to an athletic meal.
- **Office Cantina:** On office days, select the high-protein (meat/fish) option ranging from 500-900 calories. Skip fries, double the vegetables.
- **Pizza Thursday:** Maintained as a psychological release and carb-loading event for Friday/Saturday efforts. Protein is front-loaded at breakfast and lunch to hit the daily 180g anchor before the pizza.

### C. Psychological Triggers & Behavioral Rules
- **The Trigger:** 11:00 PM dog walk causes extreme willpower depletion, dopamine seeking, and late-night snacking.
- **The Fix (The Vampire Protocol):** The dog walk is permanently shifted to 21:30. Overhead lights off at 21:00. Screens off by 22:00.
- **The Kitchen Cutoff:** The kitchen hard-closes at 20:00 (8:00 PM). No solid foods after this time to lower core temperature, stabilize HRV, and improve Garmin Sleep Scores. Cravings are met with electrolytes, water, or a fast-digesting straight whey shake (no carbs/fats) if protein targets were missed.

### D. Metabolic Baselines & Supplementation
- **Supplement Baseline:** Creatine Monohydrate (5g daily) for cognitive support against sleep deprivation and explosive power. Whey Isolate (gum-free, zero stabilizers) as the primary protein supplement to hit the 180g target without bloating. 
- **Race Fueling:** Maurten Gels are the verified and cleared race-day fuel. Zero GI distress.
- **Hydration:** Base is 2 liters of plain water. Elevated to 2.5L - 3.0L on training days, with added sodium (or Buldak) to replace sweat loss.
